The Higher Ranks

League of Legends was one of the first games I played online with friends, it was the first MOBA game I tried and, in the first months that I knew about League, I didn’t have a good internet connection. This may have limited my ability to play the game, but not my ability to watch and study it! I used to spend all day on the League site, learning about lore, gameplay, and champions skills.

First time Diamond in 2015, never lower thereafter

When I finally got a good enough internet connection, all my friends were playing LoL much better than me, but I wanted to be the best. So the routine of study went forward to be the routine of evolving. I watched replays of pro matches, read the patch notes and tried to apply my knowledge in normal games. I spent six months playing only normal games and understanding the game as a whole unit, and my work was quickly rewarded.

I loved the routine of training and get better, watching my replays and pro player's too. As I climbed the ranks, I started reaching a truly elite level and even played in a pro team.

Being able to not only play with more casual players such as my friends, but also with the highest competition my region can offer, really help me understand League as a product and as a community niche.

I love being "the guru" of my friends, adivising and helping them when they ask for guidence to climb elo, and I love being competitive and competing in the best level a casual player can get.

The highest Elo I've reached so far is Grandmaster in 2019, and altought I know I can get there again and even higher, this year I'm playing much less alone and enjoying more time with my friends and my little brother.
